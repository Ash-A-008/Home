Camila Cabello

Snapshot

Karla Camila Cabello Estrabao, born on March 3, 1997, is a Cuban-born American singer, songwriter, and actress. In 2012, she auditioned for the X-Factor and joined the group Fifth Harmony, where she was known as the lead singer. While in Fifth Harmony, Camila began to establish herself as a solo artist and released her two hit singles, “I Know What You Did Last Summer” with Shawn Mendes and “Bad Things” with Machine Gun Kelly, with the latter reaching number four on the US Billboard Hot 100. On December 18, 2016, Camila’s departure from Fifth Harmony was announced.

In 2017, Camila prepared herself for the release of her debut solo album. She scored features with artists like Cashmere Cat’s “Love Incredible,” Pitbull, and J Balvin’s “Hey Ma,” scoring the artists and herself a Grammy nomination. On August 3, 2017, Camila released her hit single, “Havana,” as a gift to her fans. Due to the single's success, she ended up announcing in an interview that “Havana” is the first official single of her debut album, scraping “Crying In The Club,” her old son, which was initially planned as her debut single. “Havana” scored #1 on the Hot 100 Billboard, #1 in the UK Official charts, and over 80 more #1's on iTunes. “Havana” also became #1 on the Official Worldwide charts, giving her a name as a solo artist and the record of holding the #1 spot for the longest time as a female artist.

In just eleven months, Camila won 18 awards, became a multi-platinum artist with over 50 certifications, sold over 17 million units, and gained over 4 billion combined Youtube and Spotify streams without an album out.

Early Life

Camila was born in Havana, Cuba, to Alejandro Cabello, her father, who was born in Mexico City, and Sinuhe Estrabao, her mother, who was born in Cuba. She also has a younger sister named Sofia. For most of her early life, Camila and her family moved back and forth between Havana and Mexico City. When Camila was six years old, she moved with her mother to Miami, Florida, as her father could not obtain a visa then and joined his family after 18 months. Camila attended Miami Palmetto High School but dropped out during 2012-2013 when she was in ninth grade to pursue her singing career. Though, later she earned her high school diploma.

Career

2012 - 2016

At the age of 15, Camila auditioned for the TV talent competition show, The X Factor in Greensboro, North Carolina, with Aretha Franklin’s “Respect,” however, her audition was not aired since the series did not get the rights for the song. After elimination during the “boot camp” portion, Camila was called back to the stage with four other girls, Ally Brooke, Normani, Lauren Jauregui, and Dinah Jane, to form the girl group Fifth Harmony. After getting third place on the show, they signed a joint deal with Syco Music, owned by Simon Cowell, and Epic Records, L.A. Reid’s record label.

In October 2013, the group released the EP, “Better Together.” Their debut single, “Miss Movin’ On,” became the highest-charting U.S X Factor single. From 2013 to the end of 2016, Camila performed on various tours with Fifth Harmony. In 2015, Camila collaborated with Shawn Mendes on the duet, “I Know What You Did Last Summer,” a song they wrote together backstage on Taylor Swift’s 1989 world tour, which immediately this song hit the Billboard Top 20. On October 14, 2016, Camila joined rapper Machine Gun Kelly and released a joint single called “Bad Things,” which reached a peak of four on the US Billboard Hot 100 songs. In the same year, Time magazine included Camila on “The 25 Most Influential Teens of 2016” list.

On December 18, 2016, Fifth Harmony announced Camila’s departure from the group, with both sides giving contradictory explanations of the circumstances. A journalist writing about Camila’s time in the group noted it is “rather uncommon for someone to stand out in a collective as much as Camila has over the past tears.”

2017 - 2018

On January 25, 2017, Camila released her debut (non-album) solo single, the Sia Furler co-wrote, “Crying in the Club,” which peaked in the Top 40 of the U.S Billboard Hot 100. She also joined Bruno Mars’ 24K Magic World Tour as an opening act for several shows and partnered with Guess clothing brand.

In early 2018, she returned with her debut full-length solo album, “Camila,” featuring production by Frank Dukes, Jarami, and others. The singles “Havana” and “Never Be the Same” made Camila the first artist to top the Mainstream Top 40 and Adult Top 40 airplay charts. By the end of the year, she had picked up several Grammy nominations and won an MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year for “Havana.”

2019 - 2020

In October 2018, Camila announced she would start working on new music after the holidays. In April 2019, it was announced that Camila would star in an upcoming film adaptation of “Cinderella.”

On June 21, 2019, Camila released “Senorita” with Shawn Mendes and the music video. The song debuted at number two on the U.S Billboard Hot 100 chart. In August, “Senorita” climbed to number 1 on the Hot 100, making it Camila’s second single to top the chart. It also earned a nomination for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance.

On September 1, 2019, Camila posted a clip on Instagram, teasing the release of her second studio album, “Romance.” It was released on December 6, 2019, and was supposed to be supported by the Romance Tour in 2020, until its cancellation due to the Covid-19 pandemic. In mid-March 2020, Camila participated in iHeart Media’s Living Room Concert for America as a benefit to raise awareness and funds for the Covid-19 pandemic.

2021 - Present

On July 23, 2021, Camila released “Don’t Go Yet” as the lead single from her third studio album, “Familia,” announced alongside the release of the single. On October 29, 2021, she also released “Oh Na Na” with Myke Towers and Tainy though it was not included in the album. In early September, Camila performed “Don’t Go Yet” and a cover of Olivia Rodrigo’s song, “Good 4 U,” which later won the iHeartRadio Music Award for Best Cover Performance.

On March 4, 2022, Camila released her song “Bam Bam,” collaborated with Ed Sheeran, with a music video accompanying it. She also debuted the song with a performance on The Late Late Show With James Corden on the release day. Camila and Ed Sheeran performed “Bam Bam” live for the first time at the Concert For Ukraine benefit at Resorts World Arena in Birmingham.

On May 9, 2022, it was announced Camila would headline the UEFA Championships League Final on May 28. On May 28, 2022, she performed “Senorita,” “Havana,” “Bam Bam,” and “Don’t Go Yet” during the UEFA Champions League Final opening ceremony.

Artistry

Camila is a pop singer. She possesses a soprano vocal range. Her debut album is a pop record influenced by Latin music. The album incorporates elements of reggaeton, hip hop, and dancehall and took inspiration from artists such as Calle 13, J Balvin, Taylor Swift, and Ed Sheeran.

Awards and Nominations

Camila has won two Latin Grammy Awards, four American Music Awards, a Billboard Music Award, five MTV Europe Music Awards, two iHeart Music Awards (including one for Video of the Year), three iHeart Much Music Video Awards, and a Billboard Women in Music award for Breakthrough Artist.
